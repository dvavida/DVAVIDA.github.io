2001: A Space Odyssey Monolith of DVAVIDA

No hidden meaning. No fantasy. No aliens. MONOLITH (Space Odyssey) is The Street. The “monolith” is simply a STREET seen from above — a path stretching forward, just like life itself. “Space” refers to the environment in which a person lives. He is not lying in a bed — he is standing, looking down at the street from above, his hand pointing toward it, as if saying, “Look… see it for yourself.” The street called “Monolith” becomes the Avenue of Life’s Odyssey. Its two defining moments mirror the human condition: the beginning and the end — sun rising and falling on the street. For humanity, the “monolith” represents the street of life: birth and aging, emergence and decline. Stanley Kubrick did not film aliens. He filmed The Street. STARGATE meaning explained - is a gateway where two summers meet. On March 1, the men’s summer arrives on the east side of the street, and on September 1, the women’s summer arrives on the west side of the same street. Masonic Two Pillars meaning explained: the left and right sides of the same street; the buildings along each side form the two pillars. Motorcycle from the movie TRON - sunrise and sunset on the same street VAV-69. The movie "Tron" is a movie about sunset and sunrise. FARA on/off no mystical PHARAO. It is sunlight, in other words, a lamp turned on. FARA (Pharaoh) is a lamp (sun). DVAVIDA-MONOLITH exist on certain streets all around the world.
